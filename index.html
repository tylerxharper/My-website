( 01 — About )
Tyler Harper Chicago, IL

I've spent my career proving the same thing in different rooms: disciplined systems beat raw talent — and the way you handle small things is the way you'll handle everything.

My foundation is in wealth management and institutional finance. But credentials are table stakes. What I actually do is build. I've designed simulation platforms, AI tooling, and automation systems that take work people dread and make it fast, repeatable, and reliable. I think in architecture: where the leverage is, where the system will break, and what to build so it doesn't.

Outside of my day work, I founded The Village — a free leadership and career acceleration program for college students — because the systems that changed my life should be available to people who don't have access to them yet. I train six mornings a week before the sun's up. Not because it's impressive, but because the discipline is the point. The standard you hold privately is the standard you bring everywhere.

I'm building toward a longer-term vision: ventures at the edge of AI and financial services that make sophisticated capability accessible at scale. The mission underneath all of it is simple — raise the floor on what's possible for the people and problems I touch.
